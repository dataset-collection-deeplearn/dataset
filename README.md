The reference wind field used in this study is generated by SOWFA (Simulator fOr Wind Farm Applications), developed by the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL). SOWFA is based on the OpenFOAM numerical framework and is widely used for large-eddy simulation of atmospheric boundary layers, wind-farm inflow characterization, and wake evolution studies. It should be noted that the dataset used in this study is neither wind-turbine wake data nor a flow field generated by the aerodynamic action of a wind-turbine rotor. Instead, it represents an upstream free-inflow wind field under neutral atmospheric boundary-layer conditions over flat terrain. The wind-turbine location is used only as a spatial reference for defining the target region and sensor layout, corresponding to a meteorological remote-sensing scenario for sparse observation and wind-field reconstruction in the turbine inflow region.
From the perspective of meteorological remote sensing, the complete horizontal wind-field slice generated by SOWFA is used as the high-resolution reference ground truth, while sparse multi-source observations, including simulated LiDAR line-of-sight wind speed, meteorological-mast velocity vector, velocity components, and pressure measurements, are used as training constraints. The validation data cover spatial grid points and 100 time steps, giving a total of 122,500 spatiotemporal samples. This setting corresponds to a typical meteorological remote-sensing problem in wind-energy applications: reconstructing a continuous, two-dimensional, unsteady wind field in the turbine upstream inflow region from limited measurement locations and heterogeneous observation types.
